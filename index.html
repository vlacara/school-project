Tracing the History of New Media in Media & Information Literacy

JOBELLE FLORES • 2025
What is New Media?

Interactive, digital, networked, and user-generated content that spreads instantly across the globe.

Key shift: From passive consumers to active creators and distributors.

Chronological Timeline

1969 – ARPANET

First packet-switching network

First message crashed after typing “LO”
MIL Impact: Birth of digital connectivity → need for source verification

1991 – World Wide Web

Tim Berners-Lee invents the Web

First website still online today
MIL Impact: Information explosion → evaluate credibility & bias

2004–2006 – Web 2.0

Facebook, YouTube, Twitter

YouTube’s first video = 19 seconds
MIL Impact: Citizen journalism, copyright, digital citizenship

2007 – iPhone

Steve Jobs revolutionizes mobile internet

More smartphones than toothbrushes!
MIL Impact: Always-on culture → privacy & digital wellness

2016–2020 – Short Video

TikTok, Reels, Shorts dominate

Attention span now 8 seconds
MIL Impact: Viral misinformation → rapid fact-checking needed

2022+ – AI Era

ChatGPT, deepfakes, AI art

90% of content may be AI-made by 2026
MIL Impact: Detecting synthetic media is now essential
